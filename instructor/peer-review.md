# Peer Review

## Recommendation: two touchpoints per project

Not every milestone boundary — that gets heavy and dilutes signal. Two
well-placed reviews per project:

1. **Light calibration after 1.1.** Low stakes. Students exchange the 1.1 play
   artifact and check one thing: does the partner's bias–variance reading match
   what their plots show? Goal is to calibrate what "good interpretation" looks
   like early, before habits set.

2. **Structured review at the 1.4 synthesis capstone.** Higher stakes, rubric-
   guided. This is where the communication competency (MS3) is exercised and
   where a second reader most improves the final artifact. Feeds the revise loop:
   reviewer feedback is something the author can act on before the milestone is
   marked.

Mirror the same structure in Project 2 (one light review mid-stream, one
structured review at its final synthesis).

## Why this placement

- Early light review builds shared standards cheaply.
- Late structured review lands where the deliverable is integrative and most
  benefits from an outside reader.
- Two touchpoints keep reviewer load sane and keep each review meaningful.

## Mechanics

- Reuse the existing peer-engagement infrastructure (D2L group-restricted
  threads / the activities review flow).
- Reviews inform the author's revision; they do **not** assign the
  Satisfactory/Not-Yet judgment (that stays with the instructor) — this keeps
  accountability with individual deliverables while still capturing the
  communication outcome.
- Provide a short review rubric for the 1.4 review (claims supported by the
  figure? comparison addresses assumptions/outputs/relationships/choice?
  reproducible?).
